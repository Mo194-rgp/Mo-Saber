About one Of Our great Writers:

Ahmed Khaled Tawfik (June 10, 1962 – April 2, 2018) was an Egyptian university professor, physician, writer, author, and translator. He is considered the first Arab writer of horror literature and is most famous in the fields of young adult literature, fantasy, and science fiction. He is nicknamed "The Godfather." His literary journey began with the Paranormal series. Although horror literature was not prevalent at the time, the series achieved great success and was well received by the public. This encouraged him to continue it, and he subsequently published the Fantasia series in 1995, and the Safari series in 1996. In 2006, he published the WWW series. Ahmed Tawfik is the author of novels that have achieved widespread popular success, most notably Utopia (2008), which has been translated into several languages and republished in subsequent years He also wrote The Singa (2012), Like Icarus (2015), and In the Rat Alley (2016). He also wrote other works, including Burnable Scraps, Mind Without a Body, and Now We Open the Box, which was published in three parts. He is also known for his journalistic writing. In 2004, he joined Al-Ahram Foundation's Al-Shabab magazine, and he also published in Al-Tahrir newspaper and several other magazines. He was also active in translation, publishing the Tremors of Fear series, which is a collection of translated horror novels, and he also translated the famous novel Fight Club by Chuck PalahniukHe, also translated the novel Dermaphoria in 2010, The Kite Runner in 2012, and Che Guevara: An Illustrated Biography in 2018. His literary activity continued alongside his medical practice, as he was a faculty member and consultant in the Department of Endemic Internal Medicine at the Faculty of Medicine (Tanta University). He passed away on April 2, 2018, at the age of 55, following a sudden health crisis. .



              

About one Of The greatest Writers in the whole Arabic world:

.Bu Fihr Mahmud ibn Muhammad Shakir (1327–1418 AH/1909–97 AD) was a renowned Arabic scholar, Egyptian researcher, and prolific writer. He developed a unique approach to literary study and appreciation of Arabic expression, both poetry and prose, in what he called "the method of appreciation," and to the study of linguistic, literary, and legal heritage. He was renowned for his originality and rigor in defending Arabic and confronting waves of Westernization.He fought many literary battles regarding the authenticity of Arab culture and the sources of pre-Islamic poetry. He won the King Faisal International Prize. His students and acquaintances called him the Sheikh of Arabic. He is the brother of the distinguished scholar and verifier Ahmad Muhammad Shakir, and the cousin of the scholar and verifier Abd al-Salam HarunSheikh Mahmoud Shaker was raised in a religious environment. His brothers did not receive a secular education, but he, being the youngest of his brothers, devoted himself to a secular education. He received his early education at Al-Walda Umm Abbas School in Cairo in 1916, then, after the 1919 revolution, he moved to Al-Qarbia School in Darb Al-Jammamiz. There, he was greatly influenced by English lessons because of their interest in it and because it was new to him.Since he spent a lot of time at Al-Azhar Mosque, he heard poetry even though he didn’t know what poetry was!! It is worth mentioning that he memorized the entire collection of poems by Al-Mutanabbi during that period. In 1921, he entered the Khedivial Secondary School to join the science department and become interested in studying mathematics. At university, Shaker listened to Taha Hussein's lectures on pre-Islamic poetry, which became known as the book "On Pre-Islamic Poetry."
